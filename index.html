Selection Strategy

These are the controllable things that can improve your odds without trying to game the process.

  • Apply early enough to get good interview, tour, and child-visit slots.
  • Ask CCDS early how they handle ISAAGNY reports and what dates they need from you.
  • Prepare three specific stories: curiosity, kindness or friendship, and frustration or resilience.
  • Use school-specific reasons tied to the program, not prestige or generic "best school" language.
  • Let your son stay natural in visits. Practice separation, listening, cleanup, and answering adults, not memorized answers.
  • Do not send extra letters unless a school explicitly allows them. Browning specifically says not to send personal or first-choice letters.
